EXO's History

Early Years

EXO was formed by SM Entertainment and debuted on April 8, 2012, with their extended play (EP) "Mama." The group originally consisted of 12 members, divided into two subunits: EXO-K for the Korean market and EXO-M for the Chinese market.

Breakthrough and Success

EXO's breakthrough came with their first studio album, "XOXO," which included the hit single "Growl." The album became a million-seller, and the song "Growl" achieved immense popularity.

Member Changes

Over the years, EXO underwent member changes. Some members, such as Kris, Luhan, and Tao, left the group, while others continued to pursue their careers as a smaller group.

Global Recognition

EXO's music and performances gained global recognition, leading to successful world tours in various countries. Their albums consistently topped charts not only in South Korea but also internationally.

Solo Careers

Several EXO members ventured into successful solo careers, releasing solo albums, participating in acting roles, and pursuing individual projects while continuing to promote as a group.

Recent Years

In recent years, EXO continued to release music and engage with their dedicated fanbase, EXO-L. Their influence on the K-Pop industry and global music scene remained significant.

Future Plans

Fans eagerly anticipate EXO's future plans, including group comebacks and the ongoing success of their individual members.
